The goal of our experiment is to try to find the most effective way to reduce screen time due to the negative mental health effects a prolonged exposure to social media on anxiety, depression, and productivity. We targeted two secondary outcomes to find which optimally addresses our behavioral problem: the potential lack of knowledge on how to reduce screen time or targeting the beliefs people may have on how social media impacts the body and mind. We will have one treatment and control email, with the treatment targeting knowledge and beliefs and control having no intervention. Our results shows a significant treated estimate (2.45), but our small sample size undermined the significance of the estimate. Since we cannot fully analyze our given data to its potential, we can only assume to adopt neither video treatments.
