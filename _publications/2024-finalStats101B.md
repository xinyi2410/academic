title: "Examining the Effects of Exercise and Dark Chocolate Consumption on Blood Endorphin Levels: A Virtual Study Across Age Groups (2024)"

Investigated the combined effects of exercise and varying cocoa content in chocolate on endorphin levels in women across different age groups. Using a two-way randomized block design, 162 virtual participants from The Islands online resource were divided into six treatment conditions involving combinations of no exercise or light jogging and consuming no chocolate, chocolate with 40% cocoa, or chocolate with 99% cocoa. Blood endorphin levels were measured after the treatments. ANOVA results revealed significant individual effects of both exercise and chocolate on endorphin levels (p = 0.009 and p < 2.44e-45, respectively), though no significant interaction between the two factors or age-related differences was observed. Tukey’s HSD test further supported the independent influence of exercise and chocolate. While no interaction effect or age-based differences were found, the study reinforces the benefits of exercise and dark chocolate on endorphin levels.
